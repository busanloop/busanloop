The lounge on the 22nd floor of BIFC2 does not have to remain a simple resting area. It can be shaped into a true third place, where people encounter one another by chance, return over time, and slowly form meaningful relationships.

To make that possible, the first essential element is an open common table zone. Positioned at the center of the lounge, this area should encourage natural eye contact, brief conversations, and casual moments of connection. A combination of long shared tables and smaller round tables would allow even solo visitors to stay comfortably without feeling exposed, while also leaving room for loose and spontaneous interaction to begin.

Second, a quiet one-person retreat zone can offer a very different kind of value. Window-side seating or softly lit bar tables can create a space where individuals can sit alone, recover, and recharge. Before any conversation begins, people often need a sense of emotional ease and comfort. This kind of zone makes that possible by supporting presence without pressure.

Third, a micro-program zone can serve as a flexible setting for short-format gatherings such as book talks, lunchtime seminars, or small networking sessions. These compact programs can turn the lounge into a place where relationships do not just happen accidentally, but are actively given a starting point. It is here that connection becomes visible and real.

Fourth, a community board zone can provide a subtle invitation to participate. Through a bulletin board or digital screen, the lounge can share event schedules, interest-based meetups, light questions, and simple calls for involvement. For strangers, these prompts can become the perfect reason to start a conversation that might not otherwise happen.

Fifth, a lounge host desk zone can function as the operational heart of the space. It can welcome first-time visitors, introduce people to programs, and help bridge connections between individuals. A space becomes truly social not only through design, but through the presence of someone or something that gently facilitates participation.

Taken together, these five components go far beyond making the lounge look attractive. They can transform the BIFC2 22nd floor lounge into a living social infrastructure, a place where trust, familiarity, and relationships are built over time between people who may have once been strangers.
